Assessment

  • A is safe and correct. It reads at all sizes. The risk: it looks like many other chevron logos.
  • B tells a better story (waterline = signal surface) but the teal line becomes a smudge at 16px.
  • C is the strongest candidate. The split gap makes it unique and directly maps to Keel's core idea: isolated, contained tasks. The gap creates visual tension that a plain chevron does not have.
  • D is the weakest at small sizes. The outline collapses into a fuzzy triangle at 16px. Better for display contexts only.
