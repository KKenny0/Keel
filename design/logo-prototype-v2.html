Assessment

  • Ca is the pure blade form. Clean, sharp, works at all sizes. The go-to if you want zero risk.
  • Cb adds organic curves. Looks like forged metal at large sizes, but curves collapse at 16px into a fuzzy blob. For favicon use Ca instead.
  • Cc is the most aggressive. Thinner blades, wider gap, more visual tension. Reads well at 32px+ but the thin shapes lose mass at 16px. Best for display contexts (website header, documentation, merch).
  • Cd is Ca with a teal accent in the gap. The accent adds a "signal inside" narrative and a color memory hook. At 16px the teal line is barely visible, which is fine. At 32px+ it adds a distinctive detail that separates this from any generic chevron.
  • Recommendation: Cd for the primary mark (best balance of distinctiveness + scalability), with Ca as the simplified favicon/monochrome fallback.
